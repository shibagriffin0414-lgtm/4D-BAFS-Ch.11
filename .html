Chapter 11: Depreciation of Non-Current Assets

Chapter Digest

1. Introduction to Depreciation

Non-current assets provide long-term benefits (>1 accounting period). Under accrual accounting, the cost is not fully expensed in the acquisition year. Instead, it is allocated via depreciation over its useful life. Applies only to tangible assets.

2. Capital vs. Revenue Expenditure

Capital Expenditure provides long-term benefits and is capitalized. It includes the purchase price and directly attributable costs. Revenue Expenditure provides short-term benefits like rent and regular repairs. Exception: Repairs required before an asset is ready for use are capitalized.

3. Depreciation Methods

1. Straight-Line: Constant charge. Formula: (Cost - Residual) / Useful Life.

2. Reducing-Balance: Diminishing charge. Formula: Net Book Value (NBV) × Fixed %.

3. Usage-Based: Varies with output. Formula: Rate per unit × Actual Output.

4. Accounting Entries

To record: Dr Depreciation | Cr Accumulated Depreciation.
To transfer at year-end: Dr Profit & Loss | Cr Depreciation.
